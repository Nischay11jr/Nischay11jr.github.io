I was born in New Delhi on a November morning in 2003. In 2022, with a one-way ticket and a half-formed sense of what came next, I moved across the world to Cork, Ireland, to study Computer Science.

AI used to be a fantasy to me, a sci-fi idea about machines that thought and talked. The obsession turned real one afternoon, building a website about myself, when I got stuck on a bug for fifteen minutes. I asked ChatGPT. It fixed it, explained it, and from then on it stopped being a tool, instead it became something closer to a friend. The elegance of how it speaks comes from nothing more than a model predicting the next character, the next word. Knowing that makes it stranger and more compelling, not less. We've gone from imagining this to living inside it, different AIs for different tasks, every answer one prompt away instead of buried in a hundred pages. The pace since AI industry shipped has been remarkable. I want to be inside that pace, not outside watching it.

Coming from Delhi to Cork flipped my world. Delhi is loud, packed, layered with a culture I grew up inside; Cork is quieter, smaller, lived more on your own terms. The change was drastic, but I'm glad I made the call to leave home. Being on my own taught me what I actually want, what I actually like and don't, what consistency and discipline mean when there's no one structuring your day. Every project I've shipped started here; no one directing me, no one's blueprint to follow. I'm in my own boat now.

The goal is to build something useful for the people today, for the people tomorrow. If that work eventually carries the title AI Engineer or AI Architect under someone else's roof, fine. The title isn't the point; the work is. For now I'm freelancing, building my own things, lining up the next ones. The point of building isn't building. It's leaving something behind worth using.

"Build things of value to give back to the community."

Can AI Replace Software Engineers?

Role · Researcher & Builder
Type · Final Year Project · BSc CS, UCC
Supervisor · Prof. Utz Roedig
Stack · Next.js 16, TypeScript, Prisma, JWT

A controlled experiment in whether AI can actually replace software engineers or just look like it can. Four tools: ChatGPT, Claude Code, Cursor, and Firebase Studio where each built the same full-stack retail web app from scratch. Same 17 tasks. Same prompts. Same stack. Judged by 338 automated tests and five independent human evaluators.

The numbers told a story. Claude Code finished in 160 minutes with zero manual fixes. ChatGPT took 249 and needed 27. Firebase Studio passed every test but required 78 manual corrections to get there, perfect scores on paper, lowest usability rating in the room. The same async cookies() bug surfaced independently across three of the four tools. Possibly, a fingerprint of stale training data, not a tool failure.

The takeaway: AI tools are force multipliers, not replacements. The boundary lives at integration, not generation. The developer's job is shifting i.e. less typing, more architecting, auditing, and catching what the model couldn't see.
